Jupiter is the largest planet in our Solar System and the fifth planet from the Sun. It is a gas giant made mostly of hydrogen and helium and is known for its massive size and powerful storms.

Basic Facts

  • Diameter: 142,984 km
  • Distance from Sun: 778 million km
  • Length of Day: 9.9 hours (fastest spinning planet)
  • Length of Year: 11.86 Earth years
  • Planet Type: Gas Giant
  • Number of Known Moons: 90+ moons

Structure & Composition

Jupiter does not have a solid surface like Earth. It is mostly made of hydrogen and helium gases. Scientists believe it may have a small rocky core at its center surrounded by metallic hydrogen.
